waveform-playlist (legacy fork) — latency compensation demo

Recording through headphones and a mic always lands late — delayed by the device's round-trip audio latency.

  • You'll hear it as a "flam" against the guide track.
  • You'll see it as a waveform offset.

This demo proves the fix: measure that round-trip latency with the <latency-test> component, then shift the next recording's start by that amount to remove the offset.

Follow the steps below in order.

1. Record (uncalibrated)

Record the metronome guide track through your mic, with no compensation applied.

2. Play it back

Listen for the late "flam" against the guide track and look at the waveform offset.

3. Measure latency

4. Mute the uncalibrated track

Use that track's own mute button in the playlist below so only the calibrated take plays next.

5. Record (calibrated)

Record again — this time the measured latency shifts the take into alignment. Play it back to compare.
